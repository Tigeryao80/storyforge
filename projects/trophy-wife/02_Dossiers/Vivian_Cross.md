# Vivian Cross — Character Dossier

## Background

- Grew up in foster care in the Bronx after her mother disappeared when she was 7
- Joined the FBI at 23 — youngest recruit in her academy class
- Specialized in financial crimes and human trafficking investigations
- Has been undercover before (two prior operations, both successful)
- This is her first long-term deep cover assignment

## Physical

- 5'7", athletic build from years of combat training
- Dark hair (dyed — natural color is blonde, which she hides for cover)
- Green eyes — her most memorable feature, which she downplays with contacts on jobs
- Scar on her left shoulder blade from a childhood accident she won't discuss
- Dresses in expensive, understated clothing for the role — she's uncomfortable in it

## Personality

- **Surface:** Charming, adaptable, reads rooms instantly. Can become whoever she needs to be.
- **Beneath:** Deeply guarded. Trust issues from foster care. Uses control as a coping mechanism.
- **Fatal flaw:** She believes she can control any situation if she's smart enough. She's wrong.
- **Desire:** To belong somewhere. To be chosen, not just used.
- **Fear:** That she's become so good at pretending she doesn't know who she really is anymore.

## Skills

- Fluent in Mandarin and conversational Russian
- Expert in financial forensics and digital surveillance
- Trained in Krav Maga and Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu
- Can read microexpressions (trained by the Bureau)
- Excellent at compartmentalizing emotions — until she can't

## In the Story

- Enters the marriage believing she's in control
- Slowly realizes he's been three steps ahead the entire time
- Her attraction to him is the first thing she can't compartmentalize
- The sex scenes are where her control breaks down — and where she discovers who she actually is
- By the end, she has to choose between the mission and the man — and neither choice is clean

## Voice Notes

- First person POV chapters: sharp, observant, darkly funny internal monologue
- She notices details others miss — a skill that becomes a curse when she starts noticing *him*
- Her internal voice gets less controlled as the story progresses — the prose loosens as she unravels
