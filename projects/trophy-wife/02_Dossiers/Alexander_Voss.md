# Alexander Voss — Character Dossier

## Background

- Born in Zurich to old European money — his family's fortune dates back to banking in the 1800s
- MIT at 16, PhD in computer science at 19
- Built his first billion by 25 (AI-driven financial modeling)
- His company, Voss Technologies, is valued at $40B — legitimate on the surface
- His younger sister, Elena, was trafficked when she was 16. She survived. It changed everything.

## Physical

- 6'2", lean and precise — moves like someone who's trained in combat but doesn't need to prove it
- Dark hair, grey eyes — the kind of face that's hard to read and impossible to forget
- Dresses in bespoke suits that cost more than most people's cars
- Has a tattoo on his ribs — coordinates. He's never told anyone what they mark.
- His hands are his tell — they're always still. When they move, something is about to happen.

## Personality

- **Surface:** Polite, controlled, generous. The perfect gentleman. Media darling.
- **Beneath:** Obsessive. Strategic. Capable of extraordinary patience. Believes the ends justify any means.
- **Fatal flaw:** He believes he can save people by controlling them. He can't tell the difference between love and possession.
- **Desire:** To burn the trafficking network that took his sister — and to find someone who sees him clearly and stays anyway.
- **Fear:** That he's become the thing he's hunting. That the darkness in him is real, not just a tool.

## The Truth He's Hiding

- He's been dismantling a trafficking ring for 5 years — from the inside
- He uses his company's tech to track shipments, identify buyers, and feed intelligence to Interpol
- His methods are illegal, violent, and morally indefensible — and they work
- He's killed three people. He doesn't regret any of them.
- He requested Vivian specifically because he's been watching her career for two years. He knows she's FBI. He married her anyway.

## In the Story

- Every interaction with Vivian is calculated — except the moments when it isn't
- He wants her to *choose* him, not just submit — that's the difference between him and the monsters he hunts
- The sex scenes are where his control slips — he's more vulnerable in bed than anywhere else
- His POV chapters reveal the gap between what he shows her and what he actually feels
- The question the reader should ask: is he a good man doing bad things, or a bad man performing goodness?

## Voice Notes

- Third person limited POV chapters: controlled, precise, but with cracks showing
- He notices everything about Vivian — her tells, her patterns, the moments her mask slips
- His internal monologue is colder than his external behavior — until she gets under his skin
- As the story progresses, his chapters get more emotionally raw — the control is a performance, and the performance is failing

## Key Relationships

- **Elena Voss (sister):** The reason for everything. She doesn't know the full extent of what he's doing. She thinks he's just a billionaire with a conscience.
- **Marcus Chen (head of security):** The only person who knows everything. Former Special Forces. Loyal to Alex, not to the law.
- **The Handler (Vivian's boss):** The real trafficker. Alex has been building a case against him for years. Vivian is the final piece.
