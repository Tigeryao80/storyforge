# Trophy Wife — Chapter Outline

## ACT I: THE MARRIAGE (Chapters 1-8)
*The trap is set. Both of them think they're the hunter.*

### Chapter 1 — "The Wedding Night" (Vivian POV)
- Opens on the wedding — lavish, public, performative
- Vivian's internal monologue: she's here to destroy him
- The wedding night: explicit, controlled — she initiates to establish dominance
- He lets her think she's in control. First crack: he says something he shouldn't know about her.
- **Hook:** "You can stop pretending, Vivian. I know who you are."

### Chapter 2 — "The Morning After" (Alex POV)
- His POV reveals he's known she's FBI from the start
- Flashback: how he discovered her identity 6 months ago
- He watches her sleep — the first moment his control slips
- He makes breakfast. She's already searching his study.
- **Reveal:** He's not the target. He's been waiting for someone like her.

### Chapter 3 — "The House Rules" (Vivian POV)
- She explores the penthouse — every room monitored, every staff member loyal to him
- She finds nothing incriminating. Too clean.
- First explicit scene: he corners her in his study. Power play. She tries to dominate — he reverses it.
- She realizes he's not what the file said. He's more dangerous.
- **Psychological beat:** She's aroused and hates herself for it.

### Chapter 4 — "The Handler" (Vivian POV)
- She meets her handler (codename: MERIDIAN) at a dead drop
- He gives her the next task: access his private server
- She asks about Alex's psychological profile. MERIDIAN deflects.
- Back at the penthouse: Alex is waiting. "How was your walk?"
- He knows she left. He's letting her know he knows.

### Chapter 5 — "The First Crack" (Alex POV)
- Alex's backstory: Elena's trafficking, his years building the case
- He watches Vivian through the security feeds — not for surveillance. Because he can't stop.
- He goes to her room. Doesn't touch her. Just talks.
- First genuine conversation — both lying, both telling the truth
- **Psychological beat:** He wants her to see him. Really see him. That's more dangerous than any secret.

### Chapter 6 — "The Gala" (Vivian POV)
- Public event — they perform the perfect couple
- She meets Elena. Sees the sister's scars (emotional, not physical).
- A man at the gala recognizes Vivian from a past operation. She has to improvise.
- Alex covers for her seamlessly — he knew the risk and planned for it.
- **Reveal:** He's protecting her. She doesn't know why.

### Chapter 7 — "The Punishment" (Alex POV)
- After the gala: he confronts her about the near-miss
- Explicit scene: punishment dynamic — he takes control completely
- The scene is intense, explicit, and psychologically layered: he's teaching her that control is an illusion
- Afterward: he holds her. She doesn't pull away.
- **Psychological beat:** The line between mission and desire blurs for the first time.

### Chapter 8 — "The Server" (Vivian POV)
- She finally accesses his private server
- What she finds: not trafficking evidence. Financial records. Shell companies. Names.
- One name makes her freeze: MERIDIAN.
- Her handler's name is in Alex's files. As a *target*.
- **Act I climax:** Everything she believed about the mission is wrong.

## ACT II: THE GAME (Chapters 9-20)
*The truth unravels. The sex gets darker. The stakes get real.*

### Chapter 9 — "The Confrontation" (Alex POV)
- He knows she found the files. He's been waiting.
- He tells her the truth: he's been hunting the trafficking ring for 5 years
- She doesn't believe him. He shows her proof — surveillance, intercepted communications, bodies.
- "I'm not your enemy, Vivian. Your boss is."
- She pulls a gun on him. He doesn't flinch.

### Chapter 10 — "The Choice" (Vivian POV)
- She doesn't shoot. She doesn't believe him either.
- She contacts MERIDIAN. He tells her Alex is manipulating her. Classic psychopath playbook.
- But something in MERIDIAN's voice is off. She's trained to detect lies.
- Explicit scene: she initiates sex with Alex to "maintain cover" — but it's not cover anymore.
- **Psychological beat:** She's losing herself. And part of her wants to.

### Chapter 11 — "The Safe House" (Alex POV)
- Alex takes her to a safe house — not the penthouse
- Shows her his operation: a team of 12 people, all working to dismantle the ring
- Marcus Chen: the only other person who knows everything
- She sees the three people Alex killed. Their files. Their crimes.
- "I'm not a good man. But I'm not the monster you were sent to find."

### Chapter 12 — "The Test" (Vivian POV)
- She sets a test: she gives Alex false information to see if MERIDIAN reacts
- MERIDIAN does. Within 4 hours.
- The realization: her handler is compromised. Has been for years.
- Explicit scene: she and Alex finally stop pretending. Raw, desperate, real.
- **Psychological beat:** This is the first time she's been honest with anyone in years.

### Chapter 13 — "The Sister" (Alex POV)
- Elena visits the safe house. Meets Vivian.
- Elena doesn't know about Alex's operation — she thinks he's just paranoid
- Flashback: the night Elena was taken. Alex's helplessness. His vow.
- Vivian sees the bond between them. Understands him for the first time.

### Chapter 14 — "The Handler's Move" (Vivian POV)
- MERIDIAN realizes she's turned. Sends a team to the safe house.
- First action sequence: Alex's security team vs. MERIDIAN's contractors
- Vivian fights alongside Alex — her FBI training + his resources = effective
- They survive. Barely. One of Alex's team is killed.
- **Psychological beat:** She's chosen a side. There's no going back.

### Chapter 15 — "The Aftermath" (Alex POV)
- Alex tends to Vivian's injuries. First time he's been gentle without calculation.
- Explicit scene: slow, tender, vulnerable — the opposite of every previous encounter
- He tells her about the tattoo on his ribs: coordinates to where Elena was found
- She tells him about her mother. The disappearance. The foster care.
- **Psychological beat:** They see each other. Really see each other. It's terrifying.

### Chapter 16 — "The Plan" (Vivian POV)
- They plan to take down MERIDIAN and the network
- She uses her FBI credentials to access sealed files
- Discovers: MERIDIAN has been running the trafficking ring for 15 years. He's recruited at least 20 agents.
- The scope is bigger than either of them imagined.

### Chapter 17 — "The Trap" (Alex POV)
- Alex sets a trap: a fake meeting with a "buyer" — actually an Interpol contact
- MERIDIAN takes the bait. Sends a team.
- The meeting goes wrong. MERIDIAN himself shows up.
- **Reveal:** MERIDIAN knows Alex's identity. Has known for years. He's been using Alex to eliminate rival traffickers.

### Chapter 18 — "The Truth" (Vivian POV)
- MERIDIAN explains: he recruited Alex years ago, fed him targets, used him as a weapon
- Alex's "war" on trafficking was MERIDIAN's war on competition
- The three people Alex killed? Rival traffickers MERIDIAN wanted gone.
- Alex's face: the realization that he's been a pawn. That his sister's rescue was allowed.
- **Psychological beat:** Alex breaks. For the first time, Vivian sees him without armor.

### Chapter 19 — "The Escape" (Alex POV)
- MERIDIAN offers a deal: walk away, keep his money, keep his sister safe
- Or fight, and lose everything.
- Alex looks at Vivian. She nods.
- They fight. Explicit violence — not glorified, brutal, necessary.
- Marcus takes down MERIDIAN's team. Vivian takes down MERIDIAN.
- **Climax:** She has to choose: arrest him (FBI protocol) or let Alex handle it (justice).

### Chapter 20 — "The Choice" (Vivian POV)
- She lets Alex handle it. MERIDIAN disappears. No body. No evidence.
- The FBI investigation: Vivian files a report that buries MERIDIAN's involvement
- She's burned her career. She doesn't care.
- Explicit scene: after the storm — raw, emotional, the most explicit scene in the book
- Not about power anymore. About need.
- **Act II climax:** She chooses him. Not because she's compromised. Because she sees him clearly and stays.

## ACT III: THE AFTERMATH (Chapters 21-24)
*What happens when the game is over but the players can't stop playing.*

### Chapter 21 — "The Morning After the War" (Alex POV)
- Three weeks later. They're at his private island estate.
- The media thinks they're on their honeymoon. They're actually hiding.
- Interpol is investigating MERIDIAN's network. Alex's involvement is buried.
- But the trust is broken. She chose him, but he can't forgive himself for what he's done.

### Chapter 22 — "The Distance" (Vivian POV)
- She's no longer FBI. No longer undercover. No longer anyone's weapon.
- She doesn't know who she is without the mission.
- Alex is pulling away — not physically, but emotionally. He's afraid he'll hurt her.
- Explicit scene: she initiates, he resists, she breaks through his walls
- **Psychological beat:** She has to teach him that being loved isn't the same as being controlled.

### Chapter 23 — "The Return" (Alex POV)
- MERIDIAN isn't dead. He sends a message: Elena.
- Alex has to go back into the game. Alone. Without Vivian.
- She refuses to be left behind. They fight — the first real fight between them.
- He tells her the truth: "I don't know how to love you without owning you."
- She tells him the truth: "Then learn."

### Chapter 24 — "The End of the Game" (Dual POV)
- Final confrontation with MERIDIAN. Alex and Vivian together.
- MERIDIAN tries to turn them against each other one last time.
- It doesn't work. They've seen each other's darkness and chosen each other anyway.
- MERIDIAN is taken down — by Interpol, by evidence Vivian compiled, by Alex's years of work.
- **Epilogue:** Six months later. They're still together. Still broken. Still choosing each other.
- Final scene: explicit, tender, real — the first time they make love without any walls.
- **Final line:** "She was never his trophy. He was always hers."

## Pacing Notes

- **Explicit scenes:** 12 total, escalating in intensity and emotional depth
  - Chapters 1, 3, 7, 10, 12, 15, 20, 22, 24 = explicit
  - Chapters 5, 11, 18 = sexually charged but not explicit
  - Each sex scene must reveal character or advance plot — no filler

- **Psychological suspense beats:** Every chapter ends with a revelation or reversal
  - Act I: Vivian's assumptions are wrong
  - Act II: Alex's assumptions are wrong
  - Act III: They're both wrong about themselves

- **POV alternation:** Strict alternation — Vivian, Alex, Vivian, Alex
  - Exception: Chapter 24 is dual POV (split sections)

- **Word count target:** 85,000 words
  - Act I: 25,000 (8 chapters × ~3,100)
  - Act II: 40,000 (12 chapters × ~3,300)
  - Act III: 20,000 (4 chapters × ~5,000)
