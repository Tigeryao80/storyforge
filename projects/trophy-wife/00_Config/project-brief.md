# Project Brief

## Concept

A female federal agent marries a billionaire tech mogul to infiltrate his empire and expose his ties to an international trafficking ring. The problem: he knew who she was before the wedding. He's been playing her from the start — and the game they're playing has rules neither of them fully understands.

## Comparable Titles

- *The Risk* (S.T. Abby) — revenge + explicit + psychological
- *Twisted Love* (Ana Huang) — dark romance + hidden identity
- *Corrupt* (Penelope Douglas) — power dynamics + suspense
- *The Marriage Pact* (Michelle Richmond) — marriage as trap

## Core Conflict

**External:** She's undercover. He's the target. Every interaction is performance — except the ones that aren't.

**Internal:** She's attracted to the very man she's sworn to destroy. He's obsessed with the one woman he can't fully control. The line between mission and desire dissolves with every touch.

**The Twist:** He's not the trafficker. He's dismantling the ring from the inside. And the real leader is her own handler — the person who sent her into this marriage.

## Tone & Style

- **Explicit erotic content** — sex scenes are plot-critical, psychologically layered, and escalating in intensity
- **Psychological suspense** — every chapter ends with a revelation that recontextualizes what came before
- **Dual POV** — alternating chapters between her perspective and his, so the reader sees both sides of the manipulation
- **Power dynamics** — dominance and submission as psychological warfare, not just physical
- **Anti-slop protocol active** — no AI-typical phrases, no purple prose, human-like rhythm

## Content Warnings

- Explicit sexual content (graphic)
- Power exchange / control dynamics
- Psychological manipulation
- Captivity elements (emotional, not physical)
- Violence (off-page but referenced)
- Trafficking (plot element, not glorified)

## Themes

1. **Identity** — who are you when every relationship is a performance?
2. **Control** — the difference between being controlled and choosing to surrender
3. **Obsession** — love and madness share a border
4. **Trust** — can you trust someone who's been lying to you from day one?
5. **Redemption** — can a monster be loved into being human?

## Series Potential

Standalone with sequel hook. Book 2 could follow the handler's network being dismantled, with the couple now working together — but trust still broken.
